# Ley 2/1984, de 9 de enero, sobre utilización de los inmuebles enajenados por el Instituto Catalan del Suelo para destinarlos a vivienda

## Summary
Imposes social-function conditions on land sold by the Catalan Land Institute: purchasers must build within the specified timeframe, cannot transfer the land independently of the dwelling, and must use the resulting home as permanent residence for ten years. Breach triggers forced expropriation for social-interest reasons under the 1954 Expropriation Law, backed by municipal oversight.

## Key Provisions
- Land must be built on within the period fixed in the sale act
- Land cannot be transferred separately from the dwellings built on it
- Dwellings must be used as permanent residence for ten years
- Breach triggers expropriation for social interest (arts. 71–75 LEF 1954)
- Municipalities monitor compliance and report breaches to the Institute
- Institute reports annually to municipalities on enajenated land
