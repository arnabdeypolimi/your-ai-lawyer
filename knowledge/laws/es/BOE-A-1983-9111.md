# Ley 2/1983, de 9 de marzo, de Alta Montaña

## Summary
Establishes a specific legal regime for Catalan mountain regions and zones to combat depopulation, economic stagnation, and infrastructure deficits. Creates comarcal mountain plans as the main development instrument and enables subsidies, compensatory payments to farmers, and tax incentives to preserve population and economic activity. Identifies nine full mountain comarcas including Vall d'Aran, Cerdanya, and Pallars.

## Key Provisions
- Nine comarcas declared mountain regions in their entirety
- Comarcal mountain plans required every five years
- Plans include socio-economic study, action programs, and investment plan
- Creates Consell General de Muntanya as advisory body
- Comarcal councils represent municipalities with at least one councillor each
- Compensatory indemnities per livestock unit or hectare to mountain farmers
- Priority subsidy system for mountain zone residents
- Public participation through mandatory three-month encuesta
